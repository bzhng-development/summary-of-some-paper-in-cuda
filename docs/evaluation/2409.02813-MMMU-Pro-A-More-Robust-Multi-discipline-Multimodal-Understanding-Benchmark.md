## 1. Executive Summary
This paper introduces **MMMU-Pro**, a robust multimodal benchmark designed to eliminate shortcuts in the original **MMMU** dataset by filtering out text-only solvable questions, expanding multiple-choice options from 4 to 10, and introducing a "vision-only" input setting where questions are embedded in screenshots. The significance of this work lies in its exposure of critical gaps in current state-of-the-art models like **GPT-4o** and **Claude 3.5 Sonnet**, whose performance drops drastically by **16.8% to 26.9%** on MMMU-Pro compared to the original benchmark. By forcing models to truly integrate visual and textual information rather than relying on statistical patterns or separate OCR, MMMU-Pro provides a more rigorous evaluation of genuine multimodal reasoning capabilities.

## 2. Context and Motivation

### The Illusion of Multimodal Understanding
The central problem this paper addresses is the **overestimation of multimodal capabilities** in current Large Language Models (LLMs). While models like GPT-4o have achieved impressive scores (e.g., 69.1% on the original MMMU benchmark), the authors argue that these metrics may reflect "shortcut learning" rather than genuine reasoning. Shortcut learning occurs when a model exploits statistical correlations or superficial cues in the data to arrive at the correct answer without actually processing the intended modalities.

In the context of the original **MMMU (Massive Multi-discipline Multimodal Understanding and Reasoning)** benchmark, the authors identify two specific failure modes that allow text-only models to cheat:
1.  **Text-Only Dependency:** Some questions in the dataset are solvable using only the textual prompt, rendering the accompanying image irrelevant. A model can ignore the visual input entirely and still succeed.
2.  **Option Exploitation:** Even when an image is required, models can often guess the correct answer by analyzing the multiple-choice options alone. By leveraging pre-trained world knowledge, a model might deduce that three options are factually incorrect or that one option is the only logical fit, bypassing the need to interpret the diagram or chart provided.

Figure 2 in the paper provides concrete evidence of this phenomenon. It shows instances where **Llama-3-70B-Instruct**, a text-only model with no visual input, correctly answers complex questions about bacteriophage infection stages or historical cartoons simply by recognizing patterns in the text and options. If a text-only model can solve the problem, the benchmark has failed to test *multimodal* understanding.

### Real-World Stakes and Theoretical Significance
The importance of solving this problem extends beyond leaderboard rankings; it has profound implications for the deployment of AI in critical domains.
*   **Safety and Reliability:** If models rely on superficial cues rather than deep integration of vision and text, they are prone to catastrophic failures when faced with novel scenarios where those shortcuts do not exist. For instance, in medical diagnostics or engineering analysis, a model that guesses based on option probability rather than analyzing an X-ray or stress diagram could lead to dangerous outcomes.
*   **Cognitive Fidelity:** Theoretically, true multimodal intelligence requires the seamless integration of visual and textual information, mirroring human cognition. Humans do not process a screenshot of a graph by first transcribing the text and then separately analyzing the lines; they perceive the text and visual data as a unified whole. Current benchmarks that separate text and image inputs fail to test this integrated cognitive skill, leaving a gap between benchmark performance and real-world utility.

### Limitations of Prior Approaches
Before MMMU-Pro, the field relied on benchmarks that, while comprehensive, lacked robustness against these specific exploits.
*   **Original MMMU:** As noted, the original dataset contained questions where the image was optional or where the 4-option multiple-choice format allowed for high-probability guessing.
*   **Earlier VQA Datasets:** Prior works like **VQA**, **OK-VQA**, and **MSCOCO** focused largely on basic perception (identifying objects) or simple knowledge retrieval. They did not sufficiently challenge models with college-level, multi-discipline reasoning that requires synthesizing complex diagrams with dense textual context.
*   **Recent Specialized Benchmarks:** While newer benchmarks like **MMBench**, **SEED**, and **MathVista** have pushed towards harder tasks, the authors note that many still permit text-only solvability or fail to simulate the "messy" reality of how users interact with AI (e.g., uploading a screenshot rather than structured text).

### Positioning MMMU-Pro
MMMU-Pro positions itself not as a replacement for all existing benchmarks, but as a **stress test** specifically designed to close the loopholes identified in MMMU. It differentiates itself through a rigorous three-step construction process (illustrated in Figure 1) that directly targets the weaknesses of prior approaches:

1.  **Strict Filtering:** Unlike previous datasets that accepted all curated questions, MMMU-Pro actively removes any question that can be answered correctly by strong text-only LLMs (specifically testing against Llama-3-70B, Qwen2-72B, Yi-1.5-34B, and Mixtral-8×22B). This ensures every remaining question *requires* visual input.
2.  **Option Augmentation:** To counteract guessing strategies, the benchmark expands the candidate options from 4 to **10**. This drastically reduces the probability of a correct random guess from 25% to 10% and forces the model to distinguish between subtly different, plausible distractors generated and validated by human experts.
3.  **Vision-Only Input Setting:** This is the most significant departure from prior work. Instead of feeding the model separate text and image files, MMMU-Pro embeds the question and options directly into the image (as a screenshot or photo). This forces the model to perform Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and visual reasoning simultaneously within a single forward pass, mimicking real-world user behavior where inputs are often unstructured images.

By enforcing these constraints, MMMU-Pro shifts the evaluation focus from "can the model retrieve the right fact?" to "can the model truly see, read, and reason over integrated multimodal data?" The resulting performance drops—where even top-tier models see accuracy plummet by nearly 27%—validate the hypothesis that previous benchmarks were indeed overestimating model capabilities.

## 3. Technical Approach

This paper presents a **dataset construction methodology and evaluation framework** rather than a new model architecture, with the core idea being that robust multimodal benchmarking requires actively removing solvable shortcuts and forcing integrated visual-textual processing through a rigorous three-step pipeline.

### 3.1 Reader orientation (approachable technical breakdown)
The "system" described here is a data engineering pipeline that transforms an existing benchmark (MMMU) into a harder, cheat-proof version (MMMU-Pro) by filtering, expanding, and re-formatting questions. It solves the problem of inflated model scores caused by text-only guessing by enforcing a workflow where models must first "see" the question embedded in an image before they can "read" and reason about it.

### 3.2 Big-picture architecture (diagram in words)
The MMMU-Pro construction pipeline consists of three sequential processing stages acting on the original MMMU dataset:
1.  **Text-Only Filter Module:** Takes original questions as input, runs them through four strong text-only Large Language Models (LLMs) without images, and outputs a filtered subset containing only questions that text-only models fail to answer consistently.
2.  **Option Augmentation Engine:** Takes the filtered questions, uses a generative LLM (GPT-4o) to create six additional distractor options, and passes them through a filtering LLM (Claude 3.5) and two rounds of human validation to output questions with 10 high-quality, diverse options.
3.  **Vision-Only Renderer:** Takes the augmented questions and manually captures screenshots or photos of them displayed on screens with varied fonts and backgrounds, outputting the final "vision-only" image inputs where text is no longer provided as a separate token stream but is embedded within the pixel data.

### 3.3 Roadmap for the deep dive
*   **Step 1: The Filtering Mechanism:** We first detail how the authors mathematically define and empirically execute the removal of text-solvable questions using an ensemble of four specific LLMs.
*   **Step 2: Combating Guessing via Option Expansion:** We explain the generative and validation loop used to increase the option space from 4 to 10, reducing random guess probability from 25% to 10%.
*   **Step 3: The Vision-Only Input Protocol:** We describe the physical and synthetic process of embedding questions into images to force simultaneous OCR and reasoning.
*   **Step 4: Evaluation Metrics and Baselines:** We outline the specific scoring settings (Standard vs. Vision) and the mathematical definition of OCR accuracy used to diagnose model failures.

### 3.4 Detailed, sentence-based technical breakdown

#### Step 1: Rigorous Filtering of Text-Only Solvable Questions
The first critical design choice is to eliminate any question that does not strictly require visual input, addressing the "Text-Only Dependency" flaw identified in the original MMMU. To achieve this, the authors employ an ensemble of four state-of-the-art open-source text-only LLMs: `Llama3-70B-Instruct`, `Qwen2-72B-Instruct`, `Yi-1.5-34B-Chat`, and `Mixtral-8×22B-Instruct`. The process begins by feeding the textual component of each MMMU question (question stem and options) to these models while explicitly withholding the associated image. Crucially, the models are instructed to provide an answer even if they claim visual input is necessary, simulating a "best effort" guess based on text patterns alone.

To ensure statistical robustness and avoid penalizing questions due to random model variance, this inference process is repeated **ten times** for each model on every question. A question is flagged as "answerable by text" if a specific model answers it correctly in more than **five out of ten** trials (a majority vote threshold). The filtering criterion is strict: a question is excluded from MMMU-Pro if **at least three out of the four** models in the ensemble achieve this majority correctness threshold. This consensus mechanism ensures that only questions that are genuinely difficult for advanced language models without vision are retained. From the remaining pool, the authors randomly sample **1,800 questions**, ensuring an even distribution of **60 questions per subject** across the 30 subjects covered in the benchmark. As illustrated in **Figure 3**, this step significantly reduces the accuracy of text-only models, dropping their performance close to random chance levels before further augmentation.

#### Step 2: Option Augmentation to Reduce Guessing Probability
Even after filtering, models can exploit the limited search space of standard multiple-choice formats (typically 4 options) by using elimination strategies or recognizing subtle linguistic cues in the options themselves. To counteract this "Option Exploitation," MMMU-Pro expands the candidate set from 4 to **10 options**, theoretically reducing the probability of a correct random guess from $25\%$ to $10\%$. This augmentation is not automated blindly; it follows a hybrid human-AI workflow to maintain semantic validity.

The process initiates with **GPT-4o** generating six new distractor options for each of the 1,800 filtered questions. These generated options are then passed to **Claude 3.5** acting as a filter to remove candidates that are contextually irrelevant, logically inconsistent, or too obvious. Following this automated pre-screening, the pipeline enters a rigorous **two-round human validation phase**. In the first round, human experts review the expanded options for diversity, logical distinctness, and lack of ambiguity, correcting or replacing flawed options. In the second round, a separate pair of experts performs a cross-validation check to eliminate residual inconsistencies. During this human review, experts also re-evaluate the original question-image pairing, removing any items where the connection between the text and the visual data is weak or incoherent. This secondary quality control step results in the removal of **70 questions**, leaving a final core set of **1,730 high-quality questions** with 10 options each.

#### Step 3: The Vision-Only Input Setting
The most novel technical contribution of MMMU-Pro is the introduction of a **vision-only input setting**, which fundamentally changes how information is presented to the model. In standard benchmarks, models receive the question text as a tokenized string and the image as a separate tensor. In contrast, the vision-only setting mimics real-world user behavior where a user might upload a screenshot of a problem rather than copying and pasting the text. Here, the model receives **only an image** containing the question, the diagram, and the 10 options; no separate text tokens are provided.

To implement this, the authors do not rely solely on digital rendering, which can be too clean and uniform. Instead, they employ a mixed capture strategy to introduce real-world variability:
*   **Manual Photography:** Human annotators manually take photos of questions displayed on screens.
*   **Synthetic Artifacts:** The process intentionally varies background textures, font styles, and font sizes to replicate the diversity of real-world display environments.
*   **Dual Format Creation:** This process is applied to the 1,730 filtered and augmented questions, creating a parallel "Vision" dataset.

Consequently, the final MMMU-Pro benchmark consists of **3,460 total questions**: 1,730 in the standard format (text + image, 10 options) and 1,730 in the vision-only format (image-embedded text, 10 options). This design forces the model to perform Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and visual reasoning simultaneously within a single forward pass, testing the seamless integration of modalities that characterizes human cognition.

#### Step 4: Evaluation Protocols and Mathematical Definitions
The evaluation of models on MMMU-Pro is structured to isolate the impact of each difficulty-increasing factor. The authors define three distinct evaluation settings:
1.  **Standard (4 Options):** The original MMMU format, used solely as a baseline for comparison.
2.  **Standard (10 Options):** Text and image provided separately, but with the augmented 10-option set.
3.  **Vision-Only:** The image-embedded input with 10 options.

The **overall MMMU-Pro score** is calculated as the average accuracy across the **Standard (10 Options)** and **Vision-Only** settings. This composite metric ensures that a high score reflects both robust reasoning against distractors and genuine multimodal integration.

To diagnose whether failures in the vision-only setting stem from poor text recognition or poor reasoning, the paper defines a specific metric for **OCR Accuracy**. The authors prompt models to extract the full text of the question and options from the vision-only images. The accuracy is computed using the **Levenshtein distance**, which measures the minimum number of single-character edits (insertions, deletions, or substitutions) required to change one string into another. The formula for OCR Accuracy is defined as:

$$ \text{OCR Accuracy} = 1 - \frac{\text{Levenshtein.distance}(\text{text}_{\text{extracted}}, \text{text}_{\text{original}})}{\max(\text{len}(\text{text}_{\text{extracted}}), \text{len}(\text{text}_{\text{original}}))} $$

Where:
*   $\text{text}_{\text{extracted}}$ is the string output by the model.
*   $\text{text}_{\text{original}}$ is the ground truth text.
*   $\text{len}(\cdot)$ denotes the length of the string in characters.

This normalized score ranges from 0 to 1 (or 0% to 100%), allowing the authors to correlate text extraction capability with final reasoning performance. Additionally, the study evaluates models using both **Direct** prompting (asking for the answer immediately) and **Chain of Thought (CoT)** prompting (asking the model to "think step by step" before answering), reporting the higher of the two scores to give models the best chance of demonstrating their reasoning capacity.

#### Design Rationale and Alternative Considerations
The choice to use an ensemble of four specific open-source models for filtering, rather than a single proprietary model like GPT-4, is driven by the need for reproducibility and avoiding the bias of a single model's knowledge cutoff. By requiring consensus among diverse architectures (Llama, Qwen, Yi, Mixtral), the filter ensures that questions are removed only if they are broadly solvable by text, not just by one specific model's quirk. Similarly, the decision to manually photograph screens rather than using purely synthetic digital overlays is a deliberate design choice to introduce noise (glare, perspective distortion, font rendering differences) that better reflects "in-the-wild" conditions, preventing models from overfitting to clean, rendered fonts. The expansion to 10 options is a direct counter-measure to the statistical reality that with 4 options, a model with modest world knowledge can often eliminate 2 distractors and guess between the remaining two with 50% probability; expanding to 10 options makes this elimination strategy computationally much harder and less reliable.

## 4. Key Insights and Innovations

The MMMU-Pro paper delivers several fundamental innovations that shift the paradigm of multimodal evaluation from measuring "knowledge retrieval" to assessing "integrated perception-reasoning." Unlike incremental benchmarks that simply add more questions or harder domains, MMMU-Pro introduces structural changes to the input modality and evaluation logic that expose specific architectural weaknesses in current models.

### 4.1 The Vision-Only Input Protocol: Enforcing Integrated Cognition
The most significant theoretical and practical innovation in this work is the **Vision-Only input setting**, where the question text, options, and visual diagrams are fused into a single image (screenshot or photo) with no separate text token stream provided to the model.

*   **Differentiation from Prior Work:** Traditional multimodal benchmarks (including the original MMMU, MMBench, and SEED) operate on a **disentangled input assumption**: the model receives the question as clean, tokenized text and the diagram as a separate image tensor. This architecture allows models to process text and vision in parallel streams, often relying on the text stream to carry the entire logical burden while the image serves merely as a reference lookup. In contrast, the Vision-Only setting forces the model to perform **Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and semantic reasoning simultaneously** within the visual encoder's feature space.
*   **Significance:** This design mimics the human cognitive process of "reading while seeing," where text and visual elements are perceived as a unified scene rather than separate data channels. The results reveal a critical bottleneck: even models with near-perfect standalone OCR capabilities (e.g., >90% character accuracy) suffer drastic performance drops (up to **26.9%** overall, see Table 1) in this setting. This indicates that the failure mode is not *text extraction*, but **contextual integration**. Models struggle to associate the extracted text tokens with their spatial coordinates and visual relationships (e.g., linking a label in a chart to a specific data line) when the text is not explicitly provided as a structured prompt. This finding suggests that current "multimodal" models are actually "text-dominant models with visual attachments," lacking true cross-modal fusion.

### 4.2 The Decoupling of OCR Accuracy and Multimodal Reasoning
A counter-intuitive yet vital insight from MMMU-Pro is the **weak correlation between raw OCR accuracy and downstream reasoning performance** in complex scenarios.

*   **Differentiation from Prior Work:** Previous evaluations often assumed that if a model could read the text in an image, it could solve the problem. Benchmarks typically treated OCR as a prerequisite step; once the text was extracted, the task reverted to standard language reasoning. MMMU-Pro challenges this by demonstrating that high OCR fidelity does not guarantee correct answers.
*   **Significance:** As shown in **Figure 6** and **Table 2**, models like `LLaVA-OneVision-72B` achieve OCR accuracy scores comparable to top-tier proprietary models (e.g., ~87-88%) yet lag significantly in final accuracy on the Vision setting. Conversely, `GPT-4o` excels in both. This divergence proves that **recognizing characters is distinct from understanding their semantic role within a visual layout**. For instance, in a physics diagram, knowing the word "Force" exists is insufficient; the model must understand that "Force" labels a specific vector arrow whose direction matters for the calculation. The innovation here is the identification of **"Perceptual-Reasoning Gap"**: the inability to map recognized text entities to their visual counterparts dynamically. This insight redirects future research from simply improving OCR engines to developing architectures that better fuse textual semantics with spatial visual features.

### 4.3 Domain-Specific Efficacy of Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Reasoning
MMMU-Pro provides a nuanced analysis of **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)** prompting, revealing that its benefits are highly dependent on the disciplinary nature of the task, challenging the notion that CoT is a universal booster for multimodal tasks.

*   **Differentiation from Prior Work:** Earlier studies often reported aggregate improvements from CoT across diverse benchmarks, leading to a general belief that "more reasoning steps always help." MMMU-Pro disaggregates these results by discipline (Table 6, Figure 9), showing a sharp dichotomy.
*   **Significance:** The data reveals that CoT yields substantial gains in **structured, logic-heavy domains** like Tech & Engineering (+14.49% for GPT-4o) and Science (+8.22%), where step-by-step derivation helps manage complex constraints. However, in **subjective or interpretive domains** like Art & Design, CoT can be detrimental, causing a **17.12% drop** in performance for `LLaVA-OneVision-72B`. This suggests that forcing a linear, textual reasoning chain can interfere with the holistic, pattern-based recognition required for aesthetic or theoretical judgment. This insight is crucial for developing adaptive inference strategies: future systems may need to dynamically switch between "direct intuition" modes for perceptual tasks and "deliberate reasoning" modes for analytical tasks, rather than applying a one-size-fits-all CoT prompt.

### 4.4 The "Option Space" Stress Test: Exposing Probabilistic Guessing
By expanding the multiple-choice option set from 4 to **10 candidates**, MMMU-Pro introduces a rigorous statistical stress test that fundamentally alters the probability landscape of guessing.

*   **Differentiation from Prior Work:** Most benchmarks rely on the standard 4-option format, where random guessing yields a 25% baseline, and eliminating one obviously wrong option raises the guess probability to 33%. This allows models with modest world knowledge to "game" the system by identifying the single plausible answer among three nonsensical distractors without deeply analyzing the image.
*   **Significance:** The expansion to 10 options reduces the random baseline to **10%** and, more importantly, dilutes the effectiveness of elimination strategies. As demonstrated in **Figure 3** and **Table 1**, this augmentation alone causes significant accuracy drops (e.g., GPT-4o drops ~10.7% moving from 4 to 10 options). The innovation lies in forcing **fine-grained discrimination**: models can no longer rely on broad semantic matching but must distinguish between subtly different, plausible distractors (e.g., distinguishing between specific numerical ranges or nuanced historical interpretations). This exposes the limits of a model's precise understanding, revealing that many "correct" answers in 4-option settings were actually lucky guesses or shallow associations rather than robust deductions.

### 4.5 Error Distribution Shift: From Perception to Reasoning
Finally, MMMU-Pro innovates by quantifying a **shift in the primary source of model errors**. In the original MMMU, a significant portion of errors stemmed from basic perception failures (misreading text or misidentifying objects).

*   **Differentiation from Prior Work:** Prior error analyses often focused on improving visual encoders to fix recognition bugs. MMMU-Pro's rigorous filtering (removing text-solvable questions) and vision-only setting change the error landscape.
*   **Significance:** As detailed in **Figure 7**, **Reasoning Errors** now account for **46%** of failures in the Vision setting, a massive increase from the 26% observed in the original MMMU. Meanwhile, pure OCR/Perception errors drop to near zero for top models. This confirms that the bottleneck for state-of-the-art AI has shifted: we have largely solved the problem of "seeing" (reading text/detecting objects), but we have not yet solved the problem of "thinking with what we see." This insight redefines the research agenda, suggesting that future gains will not come from larger vision encoders alone, but from architectures that better support **cross-modal logical inference** and **spatial-temporal reasoning**.

## 5. Experimental Analysis

This section dissects the empirical evaluation of MMMU-Pro, moving beyond the headline accuracy drops to understand *how* and *why* models fail under these stricter conditions. The authors design a multi-faceted experimental framework to isolate the impact of option augmentation, vision-only inputs, and reasoning strategies, providing a granular diagnosis of current multimodal limitations.

### 5.1 Evaluation Methodology and Baselines

To ensure a comprehensive assessment, the authors evaluate a diverse spectrum of **23 multimodal models**, categorized into proprietary and open-source tiers.
*   **Proprietary Models:** These represent the state-of-the-art ceiling, including `GPT-4o` (versions 0513), `GPT-4o mini`, `Claude 3.5 Sonnet`, and `Gemini 1.5 Pro` (versions 0801 and 0523).
*   **Open-Source Models:** This group tests the accessibility frontier, ranging from massive architectures like `InternVL2-Llama3-76B` and `LLaVA-OneVision-72B` down to efficient edge models like `Phi-3.5-Vision` and `Qwen2-VL-2B`.

The experimental setup utilizes three distinct input configurations to disentangle difficulty factors:
1.  **Standard (4 Options):** The original MMMU format, serving as a baseline to replicate prior leaderboard results.
2.  **Standard (10 Options):** Text and image provided separately, but with the expanded 10-option set generated by MMMU-Pro. This isolates the impact of reducing guessing probability.
3.  **Vision-Only:** The question and options are embedded within a screenshot/photo, with no separate text input. This tests integrated perception and reasoning.

The **overall MMMU-Pro score** is defined as the average accuracy of settings (2) and (3). Crucially, for every model and setting, the authors run both **Direct** prompting (immediate answer) and **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)** prompting ("Think step by step"), reporting the higher of the two scores to ensure models are evaluated at their peak reasoning capacity.

To approximate human performance without incurring prohibitive costs for re-evaluating 3,460 complex questions, the authors derive estimates from the original MMMU human evaluation data. They adjust for the new 10-option format by simulating random guessing for questions where original human experts lacked detailed solution processes. This yields three human benchmarks: **Low (73.0%)**, **Medium (80.8%)**, and **High (85.4%)**, establishing a clear ceiling that no current AI model approaches.

### 5.2 Quantitative Results: The Collapse of Shortcut Reliance

The primary quantitative finding is a universal and severe performance degradation across all tested models when transitioning from the original MMMU to MMMU-Pro.

**The Impact of Option Augmentation**
Moving from 4 to 10 options (comparing "Standard (4 Opts)" to "Standard (10 Opts)" in **Table 1**) forces models to abandon elimination strategies.
*   **GPT-4o (0513)** drops from **64.7%** to **54.0%**, a decrease of **10.7 percentage points**.
*   **Claude 3.5 Sonnet** falls from **63.7%** to **55.0%** (-8.7%).
*   Smaller models suffer even more; **LLaVA-OneVision-7B** plummets from **42.8%** to **29.5%** (-13.3%).
This confirms that a significant portion of "correct" answers in 4-option settings was driven by the statistical ease of narrowing down choices rather than deep understanding.

**The Vision-Only Bottleneck**
The introduction of the vision-only setting (comparing "Standard (10 Opts)" to "Vision" in **Table 1**) reveals a second layer of difficulty, testing the integration of OCR and reasoning.
*   **GPT-4o (0513)** experiences a further drop of **4.3%**, landing at **49.7%**.
*   **LLaVA-OneVision-72B** sees a dramatic collapse of **14.0%**, falling from **38.0%** to **24.0%**.
*   **VILA-1.5-40B** struggles immensely, dropping from **35.9%** to **14.1%** (-21.8%).

**The Total Gap**
When combining both effects (comparing original MMMU Val to the MMMU-Pro average), the gap between AI and human performance becomes stark.
*   **GPT-4o**, the top performer, achieves an overall MMMU-Pro score of roughly **51.8%** (average of 54.0% and 49.7%), compared to its original **69.1%**. This is a total decline of **~17.3%**.
*   **VILA-1.5-40B** sees the largest relative collapse, dropping **26.9%** from its original score.
*   In contrast, the **Human Expert (Medium)** benchmark remains stable at **~80.8%**, highlighting that the difficulty increase specifically targets AI shortcuts, not inherent task complexity for humans.

### 5.3 Ablation Studies and Diagnostic Metrics

The paper includes several critical diagnostic experiments to pinpoint the root causes of these failures.

**Does Explicit OCR Help? (Table 2 & Figure 6)**
A common hypothesis is that models fail in the vision-only setting because they cannot read the text. To test this, the authors explicitly prompt models to perform OCR before answering.
*   **Result:** Explicit OCR prompts yield **negligible improvements**. For `GPT-4o`, accuracy shifts from **49.4%** (without prompt) to **49.7%** (with prompt). For `Gemini 1.5 Pro`, the change is **43.6%** to **44.4%**.
*   **Insight:** **Table 2** shows that top models already possess high standalone OCR accuracy (e.g., GPT-4o at **92.3%**, Gemini at **89.7%**). However, **Figure 6** illustrates a crucial decoupling: high OCR accuracy does not correlate perfectly with high reasoning scores. `LLaVA-OneVision-72B` has an OCR accuracy of **87.8%** (comparable to GPT-4o mini's 89.6%) but scores **24.0%** on reasoning versus GPT-4o mini's **35.2%**. This proves the bottleneck is not *reading* the text, but *reasoning* with the text once it is perceived within the visual context.

**The Double-Edged Sword of Chain-of-Thought (Figure 5 & Table 6)**
The authors analyze whether CoT prompting universally aids performance. The results are highly conditional on the domain.
*   **Structured Domains:** In **Tech & Engineering**, CoT provides a massive boost. `GPT-4o` improves by **14.49%** (from 23.23% Direct to 37.72% CoT). Similarly, in **Science**, it gains **8.22%**.
*   **Subjective Domains:** Conversely, in **Art & Design**, CoT is detrimental. `LLaVA-OneVision-72B` sees its accuracy drop by **17.12%** (from 37.53% to 20.42%) when forced to reason step-by-step.
*   **Interpretation:** This suggests that linear, textual reasoning chains interfere with the holistic, pattern-recognition processes required for aesthetic or theoretical judgment. It indicates that a "one-size-fits-all" prompting strategy is suboptimal; future systems may need to dynamically select reasoning modes based on the task type.

**Response Length and Cognitive Load (Figure 8)**
An interesting side-effect of the vision-only setting is a change in model verbosity. **Figure 8** shows that `GPT-4o` generates significantly shorter responses in the vision setting compared to the standard setting.
*   Specifically, the number of **"Analytical"** tokens (reasoning steps) decreases, while **"Descriptive"** tokens (describing the image) remain relatively high or increase proportionally.
*   This supports the "Cognitive Load" hypothesis: the extra effort required to parse the visual scene consumes model capacity, leaving fewer resources for generating deep, multi-step logical derivations.

### 5.4 Qualitative Error Analysis

The authors categorize 60 error cases from `GPT-4o` in the vision setting to update the error distribution profile (**Figure 7**).
*   **Reasoning Errors (46%):** This is the dominant failure mode, up from 26% in the original MMMU. These errors occur when the model correctly reads the text and identifies visual elements but fails to logically connect them (e.g., misinterpreting the relationship between a graph's axis and a trend line).
*   **Perception Errors (27%):** While reduced, these persist. Importantly, these are rarely pure OCR failures. Instead, they involve **spatial misalignment**, such as associating a label with the wrong part of a diagram or missing overlapping data points in a dense chart.
*   **Knowledge Errors (25%):** The model lacks the specific domain knowledge required to solve the problem, even with perfect perception.

**Specific Failure Modes:**
*   **Context Switching:** In **Figure 26** (Math), the model correctly defines the objective function and constraints individually but fails to integrate them when analyzing the geometric feasible region, leading to an incorrect optimal point.
*   **Visual Dominance:** In **Figure 33** (History), the model ignores the textual context of World War II and fixates on a visual cue related to World War I, incorrectly selecting the "League of Nations" instead of the "United Nations."
*   **Distractor Sensitivity:** With 10 options, models often select the "closest" incorrect answer rather than the exact truth. **Figure 11** shows a physics problem where the model calculates a value of 55.68mm but selects the wrong option because the augmented choices include very close numerical distractors (e.g., 55.7mm vs 50.7mm), exposing a lack of precision.

### 5.5 Critical Assessment of Experimental Validity

The experiments in MMMU-Pro convincingly support the paper's central claim: current benchmarks overestimate multimodal capabilities due to solvable shortcuts.
*   **Strengths:** The three-step isolation (Filtering -> Augmentation -> Vision-Only) provides a clean causal chain for the performance drop. The use of an ensemble of text-only models for filtering ensures robustness against individual model biases. The OCR ablation study effectively debunks the "bad OCR" excuse, forcing the community to confront the harder "integration" problem.
*   **Limitations:** The reliance on approximated human performance, while practical, introduces a potential variable; direct human evaluation on the specific vision-only screenshots might yield slightly different results due to font readability issues, though the authors argue human robustness to such noise is high. Additionally, the dataset size (1,730 questions per setting) is smaller than the original MMMU, which may limit statistical power for fine-grained sub-domain analysis, although the 30-subject stratification helps mitigate this.

Ultimately, the data presents an undeniable reality check: the gap between the best AI (~52%) and human experts (~81%) is roughly **30 percentage points** on this robust benchmark, whereas it appeared to be only ~10 points on the original MMMU. This re-calibration is the paper's most significant contribution, providing a truthful baseline for future research.

## 6. Limitations and Trade-offs

While MMMU-Pro represents a significant leap forward in robust multimodal evaluation, the paper explicitly acknowledges several limitations and inherent trade-offs in its design. Understanding these constraints is crucial for interpreting the results correctly and for guiding future improvements. The authors balance the need for rigorous "cheat-proofing" with practical constraints in data collection, human evaluation, and scope.

### 6.1 The Approximation of Human Expert Performance
A primary methodological trade-off in MMMU-Pro is the reliance on **approximated human performance** rather than direct, fresh evaluation by human experts on the new dataset.

*   **The Assumption:** The authors assume that human expert performance on MMMU-Pro closely aligns with their performance on the original MMMU validation set. This assumption rests on two pillars: (1) the core content and difficulty of the questions remain unchanged, and (2) humans possess an innate, robust ability to integrate visual and textual information, meaning the shift to a "vision-only" screenshot input should not significantly degrade their accuracy compared to the standard text+image format.
*   **The Constraint:** Conducting a new, rigorous human evaluation for 3,460 complex, college-level questions across 30 disciplines is described as "both time-consuming and costly." To circumvent this, the authors extrapolate from the original MMMU human data, adjusting for the increased option space (10 vs. 4) by simulating random guessing for cases where original human solvers lacked detailed solution processes (Section 3.1, Appendix B).
*   **The Risk:** The paper explicitly flags this in the **Limitations** section as a potential source of bias. While humans are generally robust to font variations and screen glare, the specific artifacts introduced in the vision-only setting (e.g., extreme blurring, unusual font styles, or low-resolution photos) could theoretically impede human reading speed or accuracy in ways the approximation model does not capture. If the visual noise is severe enough to frustrate human annotators, the reported "Human Expert (High)" benchmark of **85.4%** might be an overestimate, thereby artificially inflating the perceived gap between AI and human performance. However, the authors argue that the "seamless integration" capability of humans makes this risk minimal compared to the catastrophic failure modes observed in AI.

### 6.2 Residual Statistical Shortcuts and Dataset Scope
Despite the rigorous three-step filtering process, the authors concede that MMMU-Pro may not be entirely immune to exploitation.

*   **Subtle Shortcuts:** The filtering mechanism removes questions solvable by four specific strong text-only LLMs (`Llama3-70B`, `Qwen2-72B`, `Yi-1.5-34B`, `Mixtral-8×22B`). However, the **Limitations** section notes that "the dataset may still contain subtle statistical shortcuts that models can exploit." Future models, potentially trained on MMMU-Pro data itself or possessing vastly superior world knowledge, might discover new correlation patterns in the 10-option distractors or the visual layout that allow them to guess correctly without genuine reasoning. The benchmark is robust *today*, but not necessarily future-proof against models that evolve specifically to game its statistics.
*   **Disciplinary and Format Boundaries:** The scope of MMMU-Pro is strictly limited to **predefined disciplines** (six core areas, 30 subjects) and **multiple-choice question formats**.
    *   **Missing Domains:** It does not cover emerging fields outside the academic curriculum or highly specialized professional tasks that do not fit the exam-question paradigm.
    *   **Format Rigidity:** Real-world multimodal tasks often involve open-ended generation (e.g., "Describe the trend in this chart and suggest a policy"), interactive dialogue, or complex grounding tasks (e.g., "Click the button that initiates the sequence"). MMMU-Pro's reliance on multiple-choice selection, even with 10 options, simplifies the output space and does not evaluate a model's ability to generate coherent, long-form multimodal explanations or perform actions based on visual understanding.

### 6.3 The "Vision-Only" Simulation vs. True Perception
The vision-only setting is a powerful innovation, but it is a **simulation** of real-world conditions rather than a perfect replication.

*   **Controlled Artifacts:** The screenshots and photos are generated through a controlled process involving manual capture and synthetic artifacts (varying fonts, backgrounds). While this introduces noise, it may not fully capture the chaotic complexity of "in-the-wild" data, such as heavily occluded text, extreme lighting conditions, hand-drawn annotations, or dynamic video contexts.
*   **Cognitive Gap:** The paper notes that while this setting increases difficulty, it "does not fully capture the complexities of human perception." Human vision involves active scanning, context-aware focus, and prior experiential knowledge that goes beyond static image processing. For instance, a human might infer a blurred word based on the surrounding sentence structure and domain context instantly, whereas a model might fail entirely if the pixel-level OCR confidence drops below a threshold. The benchmark tests the *input modality* shift but cannot fully replicate the *cognitive mechanisms* humans use to overcome poor visual quality.

### 6.4 Computational and Data Scalability Constraints
The construction of MMMU-Pro involves significant resource overhead, which poses challenges for scalability and frequent updates.

*   **Human-in-the-Loop Bottleneck:** The option augmentation process relies heavily on human expertise. Generating 6 additional distractors per question using GPT-4o is only the first step; these must be filtered by Claude 3.5 and then undergo **two rounds of human validation** to ensure quality and diversity (Section 2.2). This intensive human labor limits the speed at which the benchmark can be expanded or refreshed. Creating a "MMMU-Pro v2" with entirely new questions would require a similar investment of expert time, making rapid iteration difficult compared to fully automated benchmark generation.
*   **Inference Costs:** Evaluating models on MMMU-Pro is more computationally expensive than on standard benchmarks. The vision-only setting requires models to process high-resolution images containing dense text, which often demands higher computational throughput and memory than processing separate text tokens and lower-resolution diagrams. Furthermore, the paper's protocol of running both Direct and CoT prompts and averaging results across settings effectively triples the inference cost per model compared to a single-run, 4-option baseline.

### 6.5 Open Questions and Future Directions
The findings of MMMU-Pro leave several critical questions unanswered, pointing to open research frontiers:

*   **The "Integration" Mechanism:** The paper successfully identifies that the bottleneck is not OCR but the *integration* of text and vision (Section 3.4, Figure 6). However, it does not prescribe a specific architectural solution. *How* should models be trained to better fuse spatial visual features with semantic text embeddings? Is the solution better pre-training data, a new attention mechanism, or a different tokenizer strategy? MMMU-Pro diagnoses the disease but leaves the cure as an open challenge.
*   **Domain-Specific Reasoning Strategies:** The divergent impact of Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting—helpful in Engineering but harmful in Art (Section 3.3)—raises the question of **adaptive reasoning**. Can models learn to autonomously detect the domain of a problem and switch between "intuitive" (direct) and "deliberate" (CoT) modes? Current models lack this meta-cognitive flexibility, and MMMU-Pro highlights the performance penalty of applying a uniform strategy.
*   **Text-Rich Image Data Scarcity:** The authors suggest that the poor performance in vision-only settings may stem from a lack of training data featuring text-rich images in reasoning-intensive contexts (Section 4). Creating such datasets at scale remains an open engineering problem. While the paper introduces a tool to generate screenshots from JSON files, scaling this to the billions of tokens required for foundational pre-training involves significant logistical and copyright hurdles.

In summary, MMMU-Pro trades **simplicity and scalability** for **robustness and realism**. By accepting higher construction costs and relying on approximated human baselines, it delivers a much more truthful assessment of current AI capabilities. The limitations identified—residual shortcuts, scope boundaries, and the approximation of human perception—are not flaws in the execution but rather boundaries of the current study that define the roadmap for the next generation of multimodal research.

## 7. Implications and Future Directions

The introduction of **MMMU-Pro** serves as a pivotal inflection point for the field of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), shifting the community's focus from chasing inflated leaderboard scores to addressing fundamental architectural deficits in vision-language integration. By rigorously demonstrating that state-of-the-art models lose up to **26.9%** of their accuracy when shortcuts are removed (Table 1), this work forces a re-evaluation of what constitutes "multimodal understanding." The implications extend beyond benchmarking, offering a concrete roadmap for model architecture, data curation, and real-world deployment strategies.

### 7.1 Reshaping the Evaluation Landscape: From Retrieval to Integration
The most immediate impact of MMMU-Pro is the **recalibration of the state-of-the-art baseline**. Prior to this work, the gap between top proprietary models (e.g., GPT-4o at ~69%) and human experts (~80%) on the original MMMU appeared manageable, suggesting that AGI-level multimodal reasoning was imminent. MMMU-Pro reveals this gap to be approximately **30 percentage points** (Human ~81% vs. GPT-4o ~52%), exposing that current models are largely "text-dominant systems with visual attachments" rather than true integrators.

This finding fundamentally changes how the field interprets progress:
*   **Debunking the "OCR Bottleneck" Myth:** The ablation studies (Table 2, Figure 6) prove that high character-level OCR accuracy does not translate to reasoning success. Consequently, future evaluations must stop treating OCR as a solved prerequisite and start measuring **spatial-semantic alignment**—the ability to map recognized text tokens to their specific visual coordinates and relational context.
*   **The End of "Clean Input" Assumptions:** By validating the **Vision-Only** setting, MMMU-Pro establishes that benchmarks relying on separate text tokens and clean image tensors are no longer sufficient proxies for real-world performance. The field must move towards evaluation protocols that simulate the "messy" reality of user inputs (screenshots, photos with glare, varied fonts), as these conditions expose fragility in cross-modal attention mechanisms that clean inputs hide.

### 7.2 Strategic Directions for Model Architecture and Training
The diagnostic results of MMMU-Pro suggest specific, high-priority avenues for architectural innovation and training data synthesis.

#### A. Developing Vision Encoders for Semantic Layouts
The paper highlights a critical divergence in vision encoder performance based on training objectives. **Table 3** shows that while language-supervised encoders (e.g., `Siglip ViT`) perform well on standard tasks, self-supervised encoders like `DINOv2 ViT-G-14` significantly outperform them in the **Vision-Only** setting (17.4% vs. 16.7% in this specific experiment, but with broader implications for feature robustness).
*   **Future Work:** Research should pivot towards **hybrid training objectives** that combine language supervision with self-supervised visual feature learning. The goal is to create encoders that do not just recognize objects or text, but inherently understand the *layout semantics*—knowing that a label floating above a bar in a chart belongs to that bar, even without explicit tokenization.

#### B. Synthesizing Text-Rich Reasoning Data
A key insight from Section 4 is the scarcity of training data that combines dense text with complex reasoning contexts. Most existing multimodal datasets separate text and image or focus on simple captioning.
*   **Actionable Path:** The community should leverage the tooling introduced in MMMU-Pro (converting JSON questions to screenshots) to generate massive-scale, **text-rich synthetic datasets**. Future pre-training pipelines must include billions of tokens where the model is forced to learn OCR and reasoning jointly, rather than relying on a frozen OCR engine or separate text streams. This "end-to-end" visual reading capability is essential for closing the performance gap identified in Figure 6.

#### C. Adaptive Reasoning Strategies (Domain-Aware CoT)
The finding that **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)** prompting improves performance in **Tech & Engineering (+14.49%)** but degrades it in **Art & Design (-17.12%)** (Table 6) challenges the "one-size-fits-all" prompting paradigm.
*   **Future Work:** This suggests the need for **Meta-Cognitive Routing**. Future models should be trained to classify the domain or nature of a query (e.g., "Is this a calculation task or an aesthetic judgment?") and dynamically switch between:
    *   **Deliberate Mode:** Activating verbose CoT for structured, logic-heavy problems.
    *   **Intuitive Mode:** Suppressing intermediate text generation for holistic, pattern-recognition tasks to avoid over-thinking or hallucinating constraints.

### 7.3 Practical Applications and Downstream Use Cases
The robustness tested by MMMU-Pro directly correlates to reliability in high-stakes real-world applications where "shortcut learning" can be catastrophic.

*   **Automated Scientific and Medical Analysis:** In domains like radiology or engineering diagnostics, models often receive inputs as PDF screenshots or photos of lab notebooks. MMMU-Pro's vision-only setting mimics this workflow. The significant performance drop observed indicates that current models are **not yet safe for autonomous deployment** in these fields without human-in-the-loop verification, as they may correctly read the text but misinterpret the spatial relationship between a diagnosis and a specific region of an X-ray (as seen in Figure 29).
*   **GUI Agents and Robotics:** The ability to "see and read simultaneously" is the core competency required for agents navigating Graphical User Interfaces (GUIs) or controlling robots via visual feedback. An agent that relies on separate text streams cannot handle dynamic UIs where button labels change or are rendered as images. MMMU-Pro provides a stress test for these agents, ensuring they can handle the visual noise and integrated text-image layouts found in real operating systems.
*   **Educational Technology:** For AI tutors grading student work or explaining diagrams, the model must understand the interplay between a student's handwritten note and a printed diagram. The **option augmentation** technique (4 to 10 options) offers a blueprint for creating educational assessments that truly test a student's (or AI's) conceptual understanding rather than their ability to guess from limited choices.

### 7.4 Reproducibility and Integration Guidance
For researchers and practitioners looking to adopt MMMU-Pro, the following guidelines clarify when and how to integrate this benchmark into development cycles:

*   **When to Use MMMU-Pro vs. Original MMMU:**
    *   **Use Original MMMU** for rapid prototyping, debugging basic vision encoder connectivity, or evaluating models specifically designed for clean, structured inputs (e.g., document understanding pipelines where text is pre-extracted).
    *   **Use MMMU-Pro** as the **primary validation metric** before deploying models in production, especially for applications involving user-generated images (screenshots, photos). If a model performs well on MMMU but fails on MMMU-Pro, it indicates the model is relying on dataset artifacts rather than genuine reasoning.

*   **Implementation Strategy:**
    *   **Baseline Establishment:** Start by evaluating your model on the **Standard (10 Options)** setting to isolate reasoning capability from OCR difficulties. If performance drops here compared to the 4-option baseline, your model suffers from "distractor sensitivity" and needs better fine-tuning on negative constraints.
    *   **Stress Testing:** Proceed to the **Vision-Only** setting. If the model fails here despite high standalone OCR scores, the issue lies in the **fusion layer** of your architecture. Do not simply swap the OCR engine; instead, focus on fine-tuning the projector or attention mechanisms that link visual patches to text tokens.
    *   **Prompt Engineering:** Adopt the **domain-aware prompting** strategy suggested by the results. Do not blindly apply CoT to all queries. Implement a classifier or heuristic to detect if a query belongs to a "CoT-beneficial" domain (Science, Math) or a "CoT-detrimental" domain (Art, Design) and adjust the system prompt accordingly.

*   **Data Augmentation for Custom Domains:**
    *   Practitioners can replicate the MMMU-Pro construction pipeline for their proprietary datasets. By using strong LLMs to filter out text-solvable questions and expanding multiple-choice options to 10, organizations can create "cheat-proof" internal evaluations that prevent overfitting to easy patterns in their specific vertical (e.g., legal contract review or financial chart analysis).

In conclusion, MMMU-Pro does not merely present a harder test; it diagnoses a systemic architectural misalignment in current MLLMs. It directs the field away from the diminishing returns of scaling text-only knowledge and towards the unsolved challenge of **true multimodal fusion**, where seeing and reading are not sequential steps, but a unified cognitive act.
